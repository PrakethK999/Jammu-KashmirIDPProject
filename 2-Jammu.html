Jammu Culture:

Jammu's culture reflects a mix of Dogra traditions and Hindu influences. It celebrates festivals like Navratri with fervor and is known for its traditional art forms, including Pashmina shawls and intricate woodwork.

Jammu Climate:

Jammu experiences a subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. Summers are scorching, with temperatures soaring above 40°C (104°F), while winters are cooler and more comfortable, making it an ideal time to visit.

Jammu Places:

Jammu boasts sacred sites like Vaishno Devi Temple, historic treasures like Raghunath Temple, and serene spots like Mansar Lake, making it a diverse and captivating destination.

Jammu Cuisine:

Jammu's cuisine is a delightful blend of North Indian flavors. It features dishes like Rajma Chawal (kidney bean curry with rice), Dum Aloo (spiced potato curry), and the famous Dogra cuisine known for its rich and aromatic gravies.
